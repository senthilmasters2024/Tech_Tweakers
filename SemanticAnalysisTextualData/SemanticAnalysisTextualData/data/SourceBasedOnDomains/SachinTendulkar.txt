Player Profile: Sachin Tendulkar

Full Name: Sachin Ramesh TendulkarDate of Birth: April 24, 1973Nationality: IndianSport: CricketPlaying Role: BatsmanBatting Style: Right-handedBowling Style: Right-arm leg spin/off spin

Career Overview:Sachin Tendulkar, often referred to as the "God of Cricket," is one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. He made his international debut for India in 1989 at the age of 16. Over a career spanning more than two decades, he amassed numerous records, including being the first cricketer to score 100 international centuries and the highest run-scorer in both Test and One-Day International (ODI) cricket. He played a crucial role in India's victory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.

Major Achievements:

Most international runs in cricket history (34,357 runs)

100 international centuries

First batsman to score a double century in ODIs

Played six Cricket World Cups (winning in 2011)

Wisden Cricketer of the Year (1997)

Bharat Ratna (India’s highest civilian award)

Playing Style:Tendulkar was known for his impeccable technique, balance, and wide range of strokes. His ability to adapt to different conditions and bowlers made him one of the most consistent batsmen. His straight drive and cover drive were particularly admired.

Legacy and Impact:Sachin Tendulkar’s influence on cricket and Indian sports culture is unparalleled. He inspired a generation of cricketers and remains an iconic figure in the sport. His humility and sportsmanship have set an example for athletes worldwide.
