In a thrilling match at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai, India defeated Australia by 5 wickets in the third ODI of the bilateral series. The match, which took place on a sunny Sunday afternoon, was attended by a packed crowd of over 30,000 fans. India's captain, Virat Kohli, led from the front with a brilliant century, scoring 112 runs off just 98 balls. His innings included 10 boundaries and 3 sixes, showcasing his class and composure under pressure.

Australia, batting first, posted a competitive total of 298 runs in their 50 overs, thanks to a solid knock of 89 runs by Steve Smith. However, India's bowlers, led by Jasprit Bumrah and Ravindra Jadeja, kept the Australian batsmen in check with disciplined bowling and sharp fielding.

Chasing 299 for victory, India got off to a shaky start, losing opener Rohit Sharma early. However, a 150-run partnership between Virat Kohli and Shreyas Iyer steadied the innings and put India on course for victory. Iyer contributed a valuable 75 runs before being dismissed in the 40th over. The match went down to the wire, with India needing 15 runs off the last two overs. Hardik Pandya hit a six and a four in the penultimate over to seal the win for India.

Fans celebrated the victory on social media, calling it one of the best performances by the Indian team in recent years. The win also gave India an unassailable 3-0 lead in the five-match series. Virat Kohli was awarded the Player of the Match for his match-winning century.
